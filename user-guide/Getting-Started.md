# Getting Started

## Introduction

Welcome to TaskFlow.

TaskFlow is a project management platform designed to help teams plan, organize, and track work throughout the software development lifecycle. From creating projects to managing sprints and monitoring progress, every feature is designed to support collaboration and improve project visibility.

This guide introduces the core workflow of the application and helps new users become productive quickly. By the end of this guide, you will understand how work is organized in TaskFlow and where to begin your daily activities.

---

## Before You Begin

Before accessing TaskFlow, ensure that your organization has created your account and assigned you to a workspace. A workspace acts as the primary environment where projects, teams, and resources are managed. Without workspace access, you may be able to sign in successfully but will not be able to view or interact with project data.

Your assigned role determines the actions you can perform within the application. Depending on your permissions, you may be able to create projects, manage work items, plan sprints, or simply participate as a team member. If expected features are unavailable after signing in, contact your workspace administrator to verify your access.

---

## Sign In

Launch TaskFlow and sign in using the credentials provided by your organization. If multi-factor authentication is enabled, complete the verification process before continuing.

After successful authentication, TaskFlow opens the workspace associated with your account. Users with access to multiple workspaces can select the appropriate workspace from the workspace switcher.

The Dashboard is displayed after sign-in and provides an overview of recent activity, assigned work, project status, and notifications requiring attention.

---

## Understand the Workspace

The workspace is the foundation of every activity performed in TaskFlow. It provides a centralized location where teams collaborate, projects are managed, and work is organized.

Most users begin by opening an existing project within their workspace. From there, they can review the backlog, participate in sprint planning, update work items, track progress using boards, and monitor project health through reports and dashboards.

Understanding this hierarchy early makes it easier to navigate the application as projects become larger and more complex.

---

## Create or Open a Project

Projects organize work related to a specific product, initiative, or team. Every work item, sprint, and board belongs to a project.

If your role includes project management permissions, create a new project by navigating to **Projects** and selecting **New Project**. Provide the required project information and configure the initial settings before inviting team members.

If you are joining an existing team, open the assigned project and review its backlog to understand the current development priorities.

---

## Create Your First Work Item

Work items represent the tasks completed by your team. Depending on the selected work item type, they may describe new functionality, defects, or user requirements.

Create a work item from within the project, provide the required details, and assign it to the appropriate team member when necessary. After it is created, the work item is added to the project backlog, where it can be prioritized, scheduled into a sprint, and tracked throughout its lifecycle using the project board.

Understanding this workflow is important because nearly every activity performed in TaskFlow begins with a work item.

---

## Continue Learning

This guide introduced the basic workflow required to begin using TaskFlow. The remaining sections of the User Guide explain each feature in greater detail and provide task-based instructions for common activities.

If you are new to TaskFlow, continue with the guides in the following order:

1. **Workspace**
2. **Projects**
3. **Backlog**
4. **Sprint**
5. **Board**
6. **Task**
7. **Bug**
8. **User Story**
9. **Notifications**
10. **Reports**

Following this sequence provides a gradual introduction to the application and mirrors the way work progresses through a typical project.

---

## Quick Start Checklist

Before moving to the next section of the User Guide, verify that you have completed the following tasks:

* Signed in to TaskFlow.
* Selected the appropriate workspace.
* Opened or created a project.
* Created your first work item.
* Confirmed that the work item appears in the project backlog.

You are now ready to continue with the remaining sections of the User Guide.
