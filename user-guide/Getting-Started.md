# Getting Started

## Introduction

Welcome to TaskFlow.

TaskFlow is a project management platform designed to help teams plan, organize, and track work throughout the software development lifecycle. It provides a centralized workspace where teams can create projects, manage work items, plan sprints, monitor progress, and collaborate throughout the development process.

Whether you are joining an existing project or setting up a new one, understanding how the application is organized will help you navigate its features more efficiently and establish a productive workflow from the start.

This guide introduces the core concepts of TaskFlow and walks through the essential tasks every new user should complete before beginning day-to-day work. By the end of this guide, you will be familiar with the application's structure, understand where work is managed, and know which guides to explore next as you become more familiar with the platform.

---

## Before You Begin

Before accessing TaskFlow, ensure that your organization has created your account and assigned you to a workspace. A workspace acts as the primary environment where projects, teams, and resources are managed. Without workspace access, you may be able to sign in successfully but will not be able to view or interact with project data.

Your assigned role determines the actions you can perform within the application. Depending on your permissions, you may be able to create projects, manage work items, plan sprints, or simply participate as a team member. If expected features are unavailable after signing in, contact your workspace administrator to verify your access.
